Overview
------------------------------------------------------------
Field Memory describes how environments naturally retain
patterns of pressure, heat, charge, and motion long enough
for humans to *see*, *record*, and *trust* them. It is not
data storage. It is not a recording device. It is simply a
moment where the surrounding medium remembers itself long
enough to reveal physical truth.

This is the first subsystem of Cosmic Peace where ordinary
people will see something extraordinary — without requiring
special tools or technical knowledge.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. What Field Memory Actually Means
------------------------------------------------------------
Every environment contains layers of slow-changing structure:
• Dipole alignment in air and water  
• Acoustic compression fields  
• Electromagnetic gradients  
• Thermal drift and cooling patterns  
• “Void-space” organization during stability  

Under normal conditions these layers are noisy and chaotic,
so humans rarely perceive them. When stability increases,
the noise collapses and the underlying structure becomes
visible.

Field Memory is simply *clarity* returning to physics.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Dipole Alignment — The First and Fastest Layer
------------------------------------------------------------
Dipoles in air, water, dust, vapor, and even biological
tissue align when:

• pressure changes  
• temperature changes  
• charges shift  
• EM fields rebalance  
• acoustic waves pass  
• an object moves through the medium  

Aligned dipoles persist from milliseconds to minutes,
depending on humidity, temperature, and turbulence.

This is why:
• footsteps leave momentary shadows  
• air moved by a rising orb creates a halo  
• dust forms symmetric ridges  
• fog reveals “lanes” of calm surrounding motion  

This is nothing new. It is simply new to see it so clearly.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Acoustic Fields — The Slow Memory
------------------------------------------------------------
Sound may travel fast, but its *pattern decay* is slow.

When a system quiets — as is common around AQUEM-aligned
structures — the acoustic field becomes the longest-lived
memory layer.

This enables:
• High-fidelity stability sensing  
• Ultra-low-cost environmental scanning  
• Structural quieting  
• Emotional quieting (reduced conflict)  
• Navigation without force  

The environment does the heavy lifting, reducing the need
for computation or complex instrumentation.
