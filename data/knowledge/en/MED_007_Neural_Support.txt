------------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------
This document outlines how TESS-128–derived environmental stabilizers
(EHAD-class devices) may support human neural function without
penetration, stimulation, or alteration of biological tissue.

This is NOT neurostimulation.
This is NOT brainwave intervention.
This is NOT cognitive manipulation.

Instead, it is:
    • environmental smoothing  
    • autonomic-load reduction  
    • sensory harmonization  
    • stability for recovering brains  
    • predictable patterns for overloaded cognition  

The focus is comfort, not control.


------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION I — WHY NEURAL SUPPORT MATTERS
------------------------------------------------------------

Many neural conditions are aggravated by:
    • sensory overload  
    • abrupt environmental transitions  
    • micro-instability in motion  
    • autonomic spikes  
    • stress cascades  
    • fatigue  

EHAD devices offer a *non-invasive buffer* that helps the nervous
system reorganize itself with less strain.

They provide:
    • smoother motion cues  
    • consistent micropressure  
    • calm backgrounds  
    • softened transitions  
    • more predictable surroundings  

The brain thrives when chaos is reduced — not removed, but shaped.


------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION II — KEY MECHANISMS (PUBLIC DESIGN)
------------------------------------------------------------

TESS-128 neural support uses three accessible mechanisms:

1. ----------------------------------------------------------
   **Micro-Kinetic Stability**
   ----------------------------------------------------------
   • A pocket or personal stabilizer subtly reduces  
     unpredictable micro-jostling (bus steps, escalators,
     subway jolts, uneven sidewalks).

   • This results in fewer reflexive corrections from:
        – the vestibular system  
        – the cerebellum  
        – spinal balance circuits  

   • Beneficial for:
        – concussion recovery  
        – vestibular disorders  
        – sensory-processing conditions  
        – neuro-fatigue  


2. ----------------------------------------------------------
   **Soft Sensory Gradienting**
   ----------------------------------------------------------
   • Not dimming or blocking the world.  
   • Just making transitions gentler.  

   Examples:
        – walking from bright sunlight into shade  
        – entering a busy room  
        – passing through sound-variable corridors  
        – navigating crowds  

   People with neural sensitivity often react not to *levels*
   but to *changes*.  
   EHADs smooth the change.


3. ----------------------------------------------------------
   **Autonomic Quieting**
   ----------------------------------------------------------
   • By reducing micro-chaos, the sympathetic nervous system  
     simply fires less often.  

   • This results in:
        – slower baseline heart rate  
        – fewer cortisol spikes  
        – reduced startle reflex  
        – diminished fatigue  

   • The device is not affecting the body directly —
     it simply removes the small stresses the brain constantly
     compensates for.


------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION III — WHO BENEFITS?
------------------------------------------------------------

The device is supportive for individuals experiencing:

• Post-concussion recovery  
• Long COVID neuro-fatigue  
• Autism spectrum sensory overload  
• ADHD environmental overstimulation  
• Early-stage dementia stress reactions  
• PTSD startle sensitivity  
• Migraine triggers from abrupt transitions  
• Motion sensitivity in elders  
• Panic susceptibility in crowded spaces  

These are *supportive* use cases, not clinical treatments.


------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION IV — CLINICAL SAFETY PRINCIPLES
------------------------------------------------------------

To ensure global medical compliance, the device must maintain:

1. **Non-Invasive Behavior**  
   Zero direct interaction with neurons or neurochemistry.

2. **No Brainwave Claims**  
   The device does not read or modify neural oscillations.

3. **No EM Stimulation**  
   EM fields remain below ANSI/IEEE C95.1 low-exposure levels.

4. **Predictability**  
   Sensory stabilization must be smooth, not dramatic.

5. **User Override**  
   Immediate shutoff must be available.

6. **No AI Personal Profiling**  
   No psychological inference or behavioral monitoring.

7. **Clinical Optionality**  
   Device works standalone, but clinicians may recommend it  
   for comfort during rehabilitation.
