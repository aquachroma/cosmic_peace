2. TESS-128 Physical Description (Public-Safe)
------------------------------------------------------------
A TESS-128 is a **spherical modular habitat shell** composed of:

• 128 triangular panels (full geodesic double-side packing)  
• internal strut lattice optimized for safety, modularity & intersection joints  
• environmental harmonization layers  
• AQUEM-compatible interior/ambient surfaces  
• optional docking junctions for multi-sphere communities  

Its purpose:
• stabilizing human environments  
• generating community calm and clarity  
• supporting predictable and resilient living spaces  
• creating community linkages (“Harmonized Constellations”)

6. Mass-Production Timeline
------------------------------------------------------------

### **Phase 1 — Semi-Automated Panel Production**
• Timeline: **9–14 months after prototype**  
• Global output: **20–40 TESS units/month/site**

### **Phase 2 — Full Distributed Production**
• Timeline: **2–3 years**  
• Output per site: **150–300 TESS units/month**  
• 12–30 cooperating sites worldwide

### **Phase 3 — Community-Driven Replication (Snellian Phase)**
• Timeline: **3–7 years**  
• Communities can build their own shells  
• CENTRAL SUPPLY shifts from “building units” to “supplying subcomponents”  
• The replication rate accelerates naturally:

> Every TESS that exists increases the rate at which new TESS units can be built.

This is the **Snellian Replication Curve**:
Replication speed ∝ community comprehension × shared capability × stability of supply.

8. Controlled Replication vs. Open Replication
------------------------------------------------------------

### **Controlled Replication (first 3–7 years)**
• Manufactured at certified facilities  
• Ensures safety during early adoption  
• Includes builder-fee model  
• Guarantees structural consistency  
• Protects communities from rushed/unsafe builds  

### **Open Replication (“fun to build” era)**
Once a community has:
• stable AQUEM culture  
• resource surplus  
• trained operators  
• reliable stockpile paths  

Then assembly becomes:
• local  
• modular  
• hobbyist-friendly  
• a point of pride  
• part of education  
• a social activation event  
• a festival-like practice  

**Think: building a canoe, a tiny home, or a telescope — but easier and safer.**

9. Economic Model: Builder Fee vs. Self-Build Reward
------------------------------------------------------------

### **Option 1 — Builder Fee Model**
If the system detects a “factory-built” TESS, the patent-holder may receive:
• licensing fee  
• builder’s royalty  
• partnership share  
• logistic support fee  
• sustainability return  

This keeps the global system funded without forcing anyone.

### **Option 2 — Self-Build Reward**
If a user builds a TESS themselves:
• reduced licensing fee  
• reduced registration cost  
• structural verification bonus  
• educational completion reward  
• community contribution credit  

This strongly encourages learning, craftsmanship, and competence.

### **Hybrid Model**
Communities mix the two:
- wealthy regions pay more (builder fee)  
- local communities offset through self-build  
- distributed fairness emerges naturally
